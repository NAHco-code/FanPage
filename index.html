Biography

  • [Born and raised in north London with two younger sisters primarily by his single mother, who is a violinist, composer, and professor at the Royal Academy of Music's Junior Academy.
  • A self-declared autodidact.
  • In 2011, he began uploading homemade, multi-instrumental content to YouTube. Which, caught the attention of Quincy Jones, who introduced him to Herbie Hancock at .
  • Was approached by MIT to create and develop musical hardware and software developing a multimedia live performance featuring six simultaneous looping stations capapable of simultaneous playback, backed by synced real-time 3D-captured video loops, projected onto a screen behind the instruments, and a vocal "Harmonizer" instrument he designed, which enabled him to perform multi-voice harmonies in real time
  • In late 2015, he debuted his first album In My Room. Which, he recorded and produced the album himself, playing every instrument. He wrote eight of the eleven tunes, and recorded + mixed the album over three months in the music room of his family home.
  • In December 2016, Collier collaborated with 150 students at MIT to produce a live concert in Kresge Auditorium titled "Imagination Off the Charts", playing alongside orchestral arrangements of his repertoire. This residency was the subject of a documentary film, which won a regional Emmy in June 2018.
  • To date, Collier has debuted: four albums; nine non-album singles; four Grammy Awards - and is nominated for three Grammy Awards in 2021, including Album of the Year for Djesse Vol. 3; and two Jazz FM Awards.]
